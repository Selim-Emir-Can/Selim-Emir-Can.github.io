About Me

Hi! My name is Selim Emir Can, and I am a Junior at UCLA studying Electrical Engineering. I was born and raised in Istanbul, Turkey but moved to Corning, New York during my sophomore year of high school school, where I then attended Corning-Painted Post High School. I am indebted to my high school teachers for shaping my broad interests in STEM, teaching me lessons applicable to situations far beyond the laboratory or classroom, and indebted to encouraging friends who, just like me, are all passionate about engineering and science. Likewise, my professors at UCLA have allowed me to develop a completely new appreciation for engineering, math and pyhsics which I am very grateful for.

I am interested in the field of mechatronics and image processing. I currently conduct research in the Robotics and Mechanisms Laboratory (RoMeLa). Outside of classes, I am involved with ASME and IEEE where I like to make personal projects and learn new engineering skills.

Outside of school, I am very passionate about sports. I love playing table tennis and basketball, running track and cross country. I also like to tutor high school and college students in my free time.
